How Does a Goal-Based SIP Work?

Goal-based investing is the process of defining a target financial amount (e.g. ₹1 Crore for retirement or a home down payment) and calculating the exact savings behavior required to hit it. By working backward from your goal, you replace abstract savings attempts with a concrete monthly action plan.

The calculation is performed by solving for the periodic installment (P) in the standard future value of annuity formula. The variables are: target wealth, annual interest rate assumption, and duration. Compounding interest compounds monthly, making it a robust reflection of recurring equity savings.

Understanding the exact monthly requirement prevents under-investing, ensuring your long-term plans are fully funded and logically grounded.

The Reverse Compounding Formula

P = FV ÷ (1 + i)n − 1i ÷ (1 + i)
  • P — Required Monthly Contribution (the value we solve for)
  • FV — Target Wealth Sum (the goal you input)
  • i — Monthly Interest Rate (annual expected return divided by 12)
  • n — Total Month Installments (years multiplied by 12)
Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an SIP Goal Planner?

An SIP Goal Calculator works backward from a target financial sum (like ₹1 Crore) to determine the exact monthly contribution needed to reach that amount over a specified period and return rate.

What returns should I assume for a 10-year goal?

For equity-heavy mutual fund SIPs in India, a long-term return assumption of 12% to 14% is historically reasonable, though actual year-to-year returns vary with market performance. For conservative goals, 8% to 10% is safer.

What happens if my actual return is lower than assumed?

If your actual market returns are lower, your final corpus will fall short of the target. To mitigate this risk, it is best to review your goals annually, top up your monthly SIP when possible, and choose conservative expected return assumptions during planning.
