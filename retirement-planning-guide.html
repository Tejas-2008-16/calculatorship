A Practical Retirement Planning Guide for Indian Investors

Retirement planning in India requires tackling two compounding forces: rising life expectancy and medical inflation. Relying solely on traditional savings accounts or fixed deposits guarantees purchasing power erosion. Here is a step-by-step framework for building a retirement corpus that outlasts you.

Accounting for Post-Retirement Inflation

If your family spends ₹60,000 per month today, and inflation averages 6% per annum, those exact same living standards will require ₹1.92 lakh per month in 20 years. Assuming a post-retirement lifespan of 25–30 years, your target corpus must sustain inflation-adjusted withdrawals throughout.

The 25x Rule for India

Target Corpus = (Annual Expenses at Retirement Age) × 25 to 30 years.
For example, if annual living expenses at 60 are ₹20 Lakhs, your baseline target corpus is ₹5 Crore to ₹6 Crore.

How SWP (Systematic Withdrawal Plan) Replaces Pension

During accumulation, you invest via monthly SIPs. Post retirement, you switch from accumulation to distribution using a Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP). SWP automatically redeems a fixed rupee amount from your mutual fund portfolio every month, transferring income directly to your bank account.

4% SWP Rate Recommended safe annual withdrawal rate from a hybrid/equity portfolio
Tax Efficiency Only capital gains portion of SWP is taxed (not principal), unlike FD interest
10% Step-Up Annual SIP top-up during work years reduces required horizon by ~5 years

Building Your Retirement Arsenal (NPS + Equity SIP)

  1. National Pension System (NPS): Provides structured equity exposure up to 75% under Active Choice, along with extra tax deduction of ₹50,000 under Section 80CCD(1B). At 60, 60% of the corpus is tax-free lump sum, while 40% goes into annuity.
  2. Equity Mutual Fund SIPs: Offer full liquidity, tax efficiency (LTCG threshold ₹1.25L/yr), and superior compounding for long-term equity growth.
  3. Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS) / RBI Floating Rate Bonds: Post 60, park a portion in government-backed guaranteed instruments for baseline essential expenses.
