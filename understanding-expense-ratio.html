Understanding Expense Ratios: The Silent Fee Eroding Your Compounding

The Total Expense Ratio (TER) is the annual management fee charged by Asset Management Companies (AMCs) to operate a mutual fund. Because TER is deducted daily from NAV prior to reporting returns, most investors never see it as an out-of-pocket invoice — yet a 1% fee difference over 20 years can consume up to 25% of your total terminal wealth.

What TER Covers Under SEBI Regulations

Under SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations 1996, TER covers fund management fees, registrar & transfer agent (RTA) fees, custodian fees, audit charges, marketing & distribution expenses, and legal overheads. SEBI mandates strict sliding-scale caps on equity schemes based on Asset Under Management (AUM):

2.25% Cap Maximum TER allowed on the first ₹500 Crore of AUM for equity funds
1.05% Cap Maximum TER allowed for AUM above ₹50,000 Crore
Daily Deduction TER is calculated as TER/365 and deducted from scheme NAV daily

The Compounding Math of a 1% Fee Gap

To understand why TER matters, compare ₹10,000 monthly SIP compounding at a gross market return of 13% CAGR over 20 years across two schemes:

Plan Type TER Net Return Final 20-Yr Corpus
Direct Plan 0.50% 12.50% ₹99.1 Lakhs
Regular Plan 1.60% 11.40% ₹85.3 Lakhs
The ₹13.8 Lakh Loss

The 1.1% fee difference between Regular and Direct plans costs the investor ₹13.8 Lakhs in lost terminal wealth over 20 years — money paid directly out of your compounding gains as distributor commission.

How to Lower Your TER Burden

  1. Switch to Direct Plans: Purchase funds directly via AMC portals or direct platforms (Zerodha Coin, Groww, PayTM Money) to bypass trail commissions.
  2. Consider Passive Index Funds: Large-cap active equity funds often charge 0.7%–1.2% TER while struggling to beat the index. Nifty 50 index funds charge as low as 0.05% to 0.20% TER.
  3. Check Tracking Error: For index funds, low TER must be paired with a low tracking error (< 0.20%) to ensure true benchmark mirroring.
