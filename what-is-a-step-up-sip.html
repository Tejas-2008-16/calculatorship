What is a Step-Up SIP? How Increasing Monthly Contributions Can Double Your Wealth

Most investors start a SIP and forget to grow it. But as your income rises every year, your investments should rise with it. A Step-Up SIP — also called a Top-Up SIP — is the single most underutilised wealth-building feature in Indian mutual fund investing, and the numbers behind it are extraordinary.

July 11, 2026 6 min read Calculatorship Editorial

What is a Step-Up SIP?

A Step-Up SIP (also widely known as a Top-Up SIP) is a variation of the regular Systematic Investment Plan where your monthly investment amount automatically increases by a fixed amount or a fixed percentage at predetermined intervals — typically once a year. This feature is now available across virtually all major AMCs in India, including SBI Mutual Fund, HDFC Mutual Fund, ICICI Prudential, Mirae Asset, Nippon India, and others.

The core idea is elegantly simple: your SIP amount grows in step with your life. Just as your rent, lifestyle expenses, and financial responsibilities grow year over year, so should your investments. A step-up SIP formalises and automates this natural progression, ensuring that a higher income always translates into proportionally higher wealth creation — not higher lifestyle inflation.

SEBI mandates that AMCs offer the top-up SIP facility as a standard feature. You can typically choose:

  • Fixed Amount Top-Up: Your SIP increases by a fixed rupee amount each year. E.g., ₹5,000/month increasing by ₹500 every year.
  • Percentage Top-Up: Your SIP increases by a fixed percentage each year. E.g., ₹5,000/month increasing by 10% per year — the most commonly recommended approach.
Nomenclature Note — Different AMCs call this feature by different names: "Step-Up SIP" (Mirae Asset, Axis), "Top-Up SIP" (HDFC, ICICI Prudential, Nippon), or "Booster SIP" (some platforms). They all function the same way — a systematic annual increase in your SIP contribution amount.

How It Works

The mechanism is automatic. When you register a Step-Up SIP, you specify your starting amount, step-up percentage (or amount), step-up frequency (annual is most common), and an optional maximum cap (so your SIP doesn't grow unboundedly if you choose percentage-based step-up).

Here's how a 10% annual step-up looks in practice, starting from ₹5,000/month:

Year 5₹5,000₹7,321

Notice that by year 20, the step-up investor is contributing over ₹30,000/month — compared to ₹5,000 flat. Crucially, this tracks almost exactly with typical Indian salary growth: someone earning ₹40,000/month in 2006 who got 10% hikes annually would be earning over ₹2.7 lakh by 2026. Their SIP has naturally scaled with their financial capacity.

The step-up investor puts in roughly ₹8.5 lakh more over 20 years — but ends up with nearly ₹2.7 crore more in final corpus. The extra investment earns disproportionately because the step-up amounts in later years (₹15,000–₹30,000/month) still have meaningful compounding time ahead of them, and they're invested at the higher end of the compounding curve where each rupee grows fastest.

Key Insight — A 10% annual increase in SIP amount can add 40–60% more to your final corpus over a 20-year horizon compared to a flat SIP. Ideal Step-Up Rate

How much should you step up? The answer is grounded in your income trajectory:

  • 10% annual step-up: The most commonly recommended rate. Matches the average corporate salary increment in India's tier-1 private sector. This is the baseline for anyone in a stable salaried role.
  • 15% annual step-up: Appropriate for high-growth professionals — tech workers, early-career consultants, startup employees with equity upside. The compounding effect is even more dramatic, but the absolute SIP amount in later years becomes very large (plan for this).
  • 5% annual step-up: A conservative choice for older investors (50+), those with slower income growth, or those already investing a large percentage of income. Even this modest step-up adds meaningful corpus over 10–15 years.
Salary Hike Connection — India's average private sector salary increment was 9.8% in 2023 and 9.4% in 2024 (Aon India Salary Survey). This means the 10% step-up SIP is not aspirational — it is designed to exactly mirror the real-world income growth of a median salaried professional. You are simply ensuring your investment growth keeps pace with your earnings.

How to Set Up

Setting up a Step-Up SIP is straightforward. Here's how to do it across the most common platforms and AMCs in India:

Directly via AMC Website or App

  1. Log in to your AMC account (SBI MF, HDFC MF, ICICI Prudential, Mirae Asset, etc.).
  2. Navigate to "Start New SIP" or "Modify Existing SIP."
  3. Look for the "Step-Up" or "Top-Up" option — it appears as an optional toggle or section within the SIP registration form.
  4. Select "Percentage" top-up (recommended) and enter "10" for 10% annual increase.
  5. Set a "Maximum SIP Amount" cap if desired (e.g., ₹50,000/month) to avoid the amount growing beyond your comfort zone.
  6. Complete the NACH mandate update if your bank limit needs to be raised.

Via Investment Platforms

  • Zerodha Coin: Supports step-up SIP via the SIP modification screen. Select "Top-Up" while creating the SIP.
  • Groww: Offers "SIP with Step-Up" directly on the fund's investment page.
  • MF Central (AMFI portal): Unified platform for all SIPs — supports top-up SIP registration across all AMCs from a single interface.
  • ET Money / INDmoney: Both support step-up SIPs with a user-friendly UI for configuring increment percentages.

Important bank mandate note: When you register a step-up SIP, your NACH (National Automated Clearing House) mandate must accommodate the maximum amount that will eventually be debited. Most platforms prompt you to set the mandate limit at 2x or 3x your starting SIP amount. If you start at ₹5,000 with a 10% step-up over 20 years, ensure your mandate allows at least ₹35,000–₹40,000/month.

Who Should Use It?

Step-Up SIPs are not just for high earners or finance enthusiasts. Here is who benefits most:

  • Early-career professionals (22–35): This is the single highest-impact demographic. Starting a ₹3,000–₹5,000/month step-up SIP at 24 instead of a flat SIP can result in crores of additional corpus by retirement.
  • Mid-career earners who feel they "can't invest more right now": The step-up SIP solves this psychologically — you commit to today's comfortable amount, and increases are triggered only when future salary hikes make them painless.
  • Parents investing for children's education or marriage: Education costs in India are inflating at 10–12% annually. A step-up SIP that also inflates at the same rate is the most aligned strategy for education corpus building.
  • Anyone with a flat SIP that hasn't changed in 3+ years: If your SIP amount is the same as it was when you started years ago, your investment as a percentage of income has declined. A step-up correction is overdue.
